4.9.2
Wrist Singularities
We can now see from Equation (4.116) that a spherical wrist is in a singular
conﬁguration whenever the vectors z3, z4 and z5 are linearly dependent. Refer-
ring to Figure 4.3 we see that this happens when the joint axes z3 and z5 are
collinear. In fact, when any two revolute joint axes are collinear a singularity
results, since an equal and opposite rotation about the axes results in no net
motion of the end-eﬀector. This is the only singularity of the spherical wrist,
and is unavoidable without imposing mechanical limits on the wrist design to
restrict its motion in such a way that z3 and z5 are prevented from lining up.
