One can see how quickly gradients gets close to 0 for inputs larger (in absolute value) than 2, and having multiple such
terms in a computation chain will likely make the corresponding terms vanish.
In other words, the gradient of the hidden state at time 𝑡will only be influenced by a few of its predecessors
{ℎ𝑡−1, ℎ𝑡−2, … } and long-term dependencies will be ignored when updating model parameters through gradient descent.
This is an occurrence of a more general phenomenon known as the vanishing gradient effect.
