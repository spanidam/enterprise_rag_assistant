9.2 Variational Auto-Encoders (VAE)
VAEs [Kingma and Welling, 2014] turn the auto-encoder into a generative model by imposing a prior on the latent
variable 𝑧, typically a normal distribution 𝑧∼𝒩(0, 𝐼). A penalty, in the form of Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence, is
added to the loss function to encourage the latent distribution to match this prior.
To generate new data:
1. sample 𝑧from 𝒩(0, 𝐼)
2. compute 𝑥gen = Decoder(𝑧)
9.3 Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN)
Proposed by [Goodfellow et al., 2014], GANs train two networks:
• a Generator 𝐺that produces 𝑥fake = 𝐺(𝑧) from noise 𝑧∼𝒩(0, 𝐼)
• a Discriminator 𝐷that predicts whether an input 𝑥is real (𝑦= 1) or generated (𝑦= 0)
