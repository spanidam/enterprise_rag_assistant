on the other hand, the entire path of the end-eﬀector can be controlled. For
example, the robot end-eﬀector can be taught to follow a straight line between
two points or even to follow a contour such as a welding seam. In addition, the
velocity and/or acceleration of the end-eﬀector can often be controlled. These
are the most advanced robots and require the most sophisticated computer
controllers and software development.
