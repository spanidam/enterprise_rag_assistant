LOSSES
We have now presented a first family of models, which is the MLP family. In order to train these models (i.e. tune their
parameters to fit the data), we need to define a loss function to be optimized. Indeed, once this loss function is picked,
optimization will consist in tuning the model parameters so as to minimize the loss.
In this section, we will present two standard losses, that are the mean squared error (that is mainly used for regression)
and logistic loss (which is used in classification settings).
