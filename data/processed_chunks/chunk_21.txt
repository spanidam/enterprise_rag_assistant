The Conﬁguration Space
A conﬁguration of a manipulator is a complete speciﬁcation of the location
of every point on the manipulator.
The set of all possible conﬁgurations is
called the conﬁguration space. In our case, if we know the values for the
joint variables (i.e., the joint angle for revolute joints, or the joint oﬀset for
prismatic joints), then it is straightforward to infer the position of any point
on the manipulator, since the individual links of the manipulator are assumed
to be rigid, and the base of the manipulator is assumed to be ﬁxed. Therefore,
