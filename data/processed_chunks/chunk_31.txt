Geometry.
Most industrial manipulators at the present time have six or fewer
degrees-of-freedom. These manipulators are usually classiﬁed kinematically on
the basis of the ﬁrst three joints of the arm, with the wrist being described
separately. The majority of these manipulators fall into one of ﬁve geometric
types: articulated (RRR), spherical (RRP), SCARA (RRP), cylindri-
cal (RPP), or Cartesian (PPP). We discuss each of these below.
