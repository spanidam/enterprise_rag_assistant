9.6 Summary
In summary, generative models offer powerful tools for modeling and sampling data distributions. Depending on the
chosen approach, they may compress information by imposing a probabilistic structure on the latent space (VAE), generate
data through competition between networks (GAN), or produce samples via dynamic and progressive processes (diffusion
and flow matching).
