GENERATIVE NEURAL NETWORKS
Generative models aim to learn the distribution of training data. This distribution can be estimated explicitly, by learning
a parametric form of 𝑝(𝑥) or the conditional probability 𝑝(𝑥|𝑦), or approached implicitly, without a closed form but
allowing sampling of new data.
Among the main generative models, we find Gaussian Mixture Models (GMM), Variational Auto-Encoders (VAE), Gen-
erative Adversarial Networks (GAN), and diffusion models. Each of these models proposes a different approach to
modeling and generating data, ranging from direct estimation of the distribution to more indirect methods based on
sampling or competition between networks.
