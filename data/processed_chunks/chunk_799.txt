A connected component is a collection of pixels, S, such that for any two
pixels, say P and P ′, in S, there is a 4-connected path between them and this
path is contained in S.
Intuitively, this deﬁnition means that it is possible
to move from P to P ′ by “taking steps” only to adjacent pixels without ever
leaving the region S.
The purpose of a component labeling algorithm is to
assign a unique label to each such S.
