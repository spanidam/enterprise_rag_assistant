Here we describe how the potential ﬁeld can be constructed
directly on the conﬁguration space of the robot. However, as will become clear,
computing the gradient of such a ﬁeld is not feasible in general, so in Section 5.3
we will develop an alternative, in which the potential ﬁeld is ﬁrst constructed
on the workspace, and then its eﬀects are mapped to the conﬁguration space.
