5.4
USING RANDOM MOTIONS TO ESCAPE LOCAL MINIMA
As noted above, one problem that plagues artiﬁcial potential ﬁeld methods
for path planning is the existence of local minima in the potential ﬁeld. In
the case of articulated manipulators, the resultant ﬁeld U is the sum of many
attractive and repulsive ﬁelds deﬁned over ℜ3. This problem has long been
known in the optimization community, where probabilistic methods such as
simulated annealing have been developed to cope with it. Similarly, the robot
path planning community has developed what are known as randomized methods
to deal with this and other problems. The ﬁrst of these methods was developed
speciﬁcally to cope with the problem of local minima in potential ﬁelds.
