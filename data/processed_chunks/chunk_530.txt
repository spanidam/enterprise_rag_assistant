7.1
INTRODUCTION
The control problem for robot manipulators is the problem of determining
the time history of joint inputs required to cause the end-eﬀector to execute
a commanded motion. The joint inputs may be joint forces and torques, or
they may be inputs to the actuators, for example, voltage inputs to the motors,
depending on the model used for controller design. The commanded motion is
typically speciﬁed either as a sequence of end-eﬀector positions and orientations,
or as a continuous path.
