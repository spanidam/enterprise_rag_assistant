9.1 Auto-encoders
Auto-encoders [Hinton and Salakhutdinov, 2006] are networks that learn to compress information into a latent space. An
auto-encoder consists of an Encoder block and a Decoder block, used as follows:

In other words, the encoder projects the input 𝑥to a latent representation 𝑧, usually of lower dimension, and the decoder
reconstructs an approximation
̂𝑥from 𝑧. This process can be seen as a generalization of PCA to the nonlinear case.
However, a standard auto-encoder is not a generative model, as it does not impose any particular distribution on the latent
space 𝑧and thus does not provide a way to sample new data.
