To cope with this problem, we can use a set of ﬂoating repulsive
control points, afloat,i, typically one per link of the robot arm. The ﬂoating
control points are deﬁned as points on the boundary of a link that are closest
to any workspace obstacle. Obviously the choice of the afloat,i depends on the
conﬁguration q. For the example shown in Figure 5.5, afloat would be located at
the center of edge E1, thus repelling the robot from the obstacle. The repulsive
force acting on afloat is deﬁned in the same way as for the other control points,
using (5.14).
